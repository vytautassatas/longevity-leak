### Molecular Mechanism

Apigenin works through multiple pathways:

**GABA-A Receptor Modulation:** Apigenin acts as a positive allosteric modulator at the benzodiazepine binding site on GABA-A receptors [(Campbell et al., 2004)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bcp.2004.07.015), enhancing chloride ion influx and neuronal hyperpolarization. Unlike benzodiazepines, apigenin shows selective binding with lower affinity, reducing addiction potential while maintaining therapeutic effects.

**CD38 Inhibition:** Apigenin inhibits CD38, a NAD+ consuming enzyme [(Escande et al., 2013)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cmet.2013.03.004), leading to increased cellular NAD+ levels. This enhances mitochondrial function and supports circadian rhythm regulation through SIRT1 activation.

**Neuroinflammation Reduction:** By inhibiting NF-κB signaling and reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6), apigenin addresses one of the root causes of sleep disturbances in aging populations [(Salehi et al., 2019)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6412475/).

Apigenin may not work if:

- **Sleep apnea is present** (requires medical treatment)

Address these foundational issues first. Apigenin works best when basic sleep hygiene is already optimized.
